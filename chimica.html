THE CHEMISTRY INSIGHT

Voices from the Laboratory: A Student Perspective

The Interview Questions

  • Why did you choose the chemistry track five years ago?
  • What was the most difficult part of these five years?
  • How would you describe the atmosphere in your class?
  • Is there a particular memory or funny anecdote you'll take with you?
  • How much time do you spend in the lab each week?
  • What is the most interesting experiment you have ever done?
  • What is the golden rule for safety in your lab?
  • What will you miss most about practical work after graduation?
  • How do you feel about the subjects chosen for the second written exam?
  • Are you worried about having external examiners this year?
  • How are you preparing for the oral exam?
  • What do you think of the recent changes to the exam structure?
  • What subject are you most afraid to face?
  • What are your plans after graduation? University or work?
  • Do you think this school has prepared you well for the "real world"?
  • Would you recommend this course to a middle school student? Why?

1. Motivation & Choice

"They explained that they had chosen chemistry because seeing the practical side during an open day had convinced them immediately."

2. Challenges & Atmosphere

"The hardest part had been balancing demanding studies with sports, while the class atmosphere remained quite fragmented."

3. Lab Life & Memories

"They recalled that digging for soil samples was their funniest memory, and they spent 10 hours a week making soap and searching for bacteria."

4. Safety & Final Exams

"They emphasized that the golden rules were safety gear and the fume hood, though they felt disappointed that Analytical Chemistry was chosen over Biology."

5. Future & Beyond

"They decided to pursue Biotechnology at university, stating that seeing processes in person made learning much more intuitive."
