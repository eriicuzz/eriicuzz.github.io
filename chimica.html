Student Interview Report:
Life in the Chemistry & Biotech Department

Insights from a Group of Three High School Students

Interview Questions

Before diving into their detailed responses, let's review the questions posed to the three students:

  • Why did you choose the chemistry track five years ago?
  • What was the most difficult part of these five years?
  • How would you describe the atmosphere in your class?
  • Is there a particular memory or funny anecdote you'll take with you?
  • How much time do you spend in the lab each week?
  • What is the most interesting experiment you have ever done?
  • What is the golden rule for safety in your lab?
  • What will you miss most about practical work after graduation?
  • How do you feel about the subjects chosen for the second written exam?
  • Are you worried about having external examiners this year?
  • How are you preparing for the multidisciplinary oral exam?
  • What do you think of the recent changes made by the Ministry to the exam structure?
  • What subject are you most afraid to face before the commission?
  • What are your plans after graduation? University or work?
  • Do you think this school has prepared you well for the "real world"?
  • Would you recommend this course to a middle school student? Why?

1. Academic Background and Motivation

The three students explained that they had chosen the chemistry track five years prior because they had all become fascinated by the laboratories during an open day. They mentioned that seeing the practical applications of science had convinced them immediately that it was the right choice for their future.

"Seeing the practical side of science truly convinced us to choose this path!"

2. Challenges and School Atmosphere

Regarding their struggles, they stated that the hardest part had been managing their time effectively between a demanding study schedule and their sports training.

"Balancing demanding studies with sports training was definitely the toughest part."

When asked about the atmosphere in their class, they admitted they did not like it very much, describing it as fragmented and divided into small groups rather than being a united team.

3. Laboratory Experience

They recalled a fun anecdote from when they had spent a day digging in the school garden to collect soil samples, calling it a refreshing break from routine.

"Digging in the school garden for soil samples was a surprisingly fun and different day!"

The students explained that they spend 10 hours a week in the lab, focusing on biology, analytical chemistry, and organic chemistry. Their favorite experiments included searching for bacteria and the saponification process (making soap).

4. Safety and Graduation Exams

Safety is a priority: they emphasized that the "golden rules" were the constant use of lab coats, goggles, and gloves, always working under a fume hood.

"Lab coats, goggles, gloves, and working under the fume hood – those are non-negotiable for safety!"

Regarding the Maturità, they expressed disappointment that Analytical Chemistry was chosen for the written exam instead of Biology, but they claimed they were not worried about facing external examiners.

5. Future Aspirations

The students confirmed they were preparing diligently for the oral interview. Looking ahead, they said they would be taking the TOLC exams soon. All three had decided to study Biotechnology at university, believing that seeing science in action makes learning more intuitive.

"We're all set to pursue Biotechnology in university; seeing science in action truly makes learning click."
